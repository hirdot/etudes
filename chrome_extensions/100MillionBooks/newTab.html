Welcome

Hi. I'm Steve, creator of 100 Million Books. I'm so glad you're here!

You're joining thousands of people across 100+ countries who discover new books on here every day.

You'll see all kinds of stuff on here: modern best-sellers & popular titles, ancient classics from far corners of the world, rare out-of-print titles that never got much attention...and so much more.

Oh, and every excerpt on here is hand-picked and painstakingly verified to make sure you see the text exactly as the author intended you to see it.

That's exactly what makes this thing special. It isn't run by a large company trying to optimize sales or clicks. Books you see are totally random—so it's kind of like browsing your favorite second-hand bookshop, but WAY bigger (and you can do it at work).

Always feel free to reach out to me directly at steve@100millionbooks.org with feedback, suggestions, or just to say hi. I would love to hear from you!

You should also check us out on Twitter, Medium, and Facebook.

Cheers,
Steve

Big Changes Coming

I happen to believe progress is generally best achieved through increasing transparency and enabling collaboration. So that's the direction I'm taking 100 Million Books in. Until now, I've run this thing like a corporation: top-down, centralized, and somewhat secretive.

For a project like this, that doesn't make a whole lot of sense, and because there's a lot of work and I'm just 1 dude, growth hasn't been great. That's not cool. So here are my goals for the next few months:

  • Open-source all client code. To invite developers to contribute features, improvements, etc. Also: when I say this service takes your privacy seriously, you shouldn't have to trust me. I hope you do, but with open-source code, you can verify that claim for yourself.
  • Post all book data publicly. To invite users to contribute corrections and new contributions. Also: I say I take bias and transparency seriously here. With all book data posted publicly, you can run all the analyses you please to verify that claim for yourself too.
  • Release a desktop program. Called Whispers! For Windows, Mac, and Linux. I've been using it for a few weeks now and like it better than both this extension and the Android app.
  • Replace annual memberships with a credits system. Pay with a contribution (see above), or chip in a couple bucks. All Pronto members will be grandfathered into this new system seamlessly.
  • Re-introduce bitcoin. Despite all the FUD, it's my belief that there's never been a more substantial innovation for the empowerment of people around the globe. This service will once again accept bitcoin.

I'm really excited to see the direction these changes take this project. I think they'll make it align better with its mission, and I think you'll find they result in a better experience all-around. I have a pretty good idea of how this is all going to work, and I'll be putting the pieces in place gradually, but I'll release more details as I can! As always, I welcome your feedback.

Steve
